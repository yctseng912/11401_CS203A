## General

This study note offers a comprehensive overview of fundamental data structures.

- [Introduction](./01_Introduction.md): Overview of data structures and their significance in computer science. *(Materials provided by the course instructor)*
- [Abstract Data Type (ADT)](./02_ADT.md): Fundamental concepts of abstract data types, their definition, and how they provide a blueprint for implementing data structures. *(Materials provided by the course instructor)*
- [Array](./03_Array.md): Comprehensive guide to arrays, their operations, and practical applications.
- [Linked List](./04_LinkedList.md): Introduction to linked lists, various types, and their implementations.
- [Comparison: Array vs. Linked List](./05_Comparison_Array_LinkedList.md): Comparison between Array and Linked List. *(Materials provided by the course instructor)*
- [Stack & Queue](./06_stack_and_queue.md): Exploration of stack and queue data structures, their distinctions, and applications.
- [Hashing](./07_Hashing.md): Deep dive into hash tables, hashing algorithms, and collision handling strategies.
- [Tree](./08_Tree.md): Complete overview of tree structures, various types, and traversal algorithms.
- [Heap](./09_Heap.md): Detailed study of heap data structure, min/max heaps, and priority queue implementations.
- [Graph](./10_Graph.md): Foundation of graph theory, representation methods, and fundamental algorithms.
- [Comparison: Tree vs. Graph](./11_Comparison_Tree_Graph.md): Comparison between Tree and Graph. *(Materials provided by the course instructor)*
- [Handwritten notes](./Handwritten_notes.pdf): Handwritten notes covering core topics in data structures, including hashing, trees, heaps, BST, red-black trees, asymptotic notation (upper/lower/tight bounds), and string matching (KMP), as well as proofs of several theorems.
- [Final overview](./FinalOverview.pdf): Notes for preparing the final exam.
